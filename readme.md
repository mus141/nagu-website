## The brand world

NAGU sells **high-status street culture in a wrapper** at an everyday price. The front-end is
hyper-visual — poster culture, anime energy, collectible IP. The back-end is a lean,
disciplined takeaway machine: 3 hero burgers, 4 sides, a compact window footprint.

**Brand enemy:** generic, soulless, corporate value fast food.
**Audience:** Gen Z & trend-aware urban young adults — digitally fluent, fast-paced, collectors.
**Archetypes:** The Culture Creator (primary) + The Jester with an Open Grill (secondary).

### Five pillars
1. **Value Smash, Asian Twist** — classic smash formats + Korean flavor + NAGU by CHAN co-brand line.
2. **High-Impact Street Visibility** — micro-windows as physical billboards; neon, raw posters, industrial finishes.
3. **Integrated Character IP** — three sibling samurai-anime mascots (Nagu, Biku, Fifu).
4. **Collectible / Loyalty** — character cards, sticker packs, pins, "stamp match" loyalty.
5. **High-Velocity Media** — every touchpoint runs like a streetwear drop, not a menu.

### The menu (canonical)
- **Burgers:** Old School Double Smash · Korean Beef Smash · Asian Fried Chicken
- **Sides:** French Fries · Loaded Fries · Kendo Chicken Bites · Sriracha Honey Glazed Shrimp
- **Premium co-brand — NAGU by CHAN:** Bulgogi Smash · Fire Chicken · Kimchi Double Cheese
- **Pricing (SR):** Burgers 18–24 · Sides 7–15 · Combos 24–30 · Kids Box 18 · Drinks 6–8

### The mascots
Three sibling samurai-anime characters, streetwear-dressed (NAGU caps, kimono/abaya jackets,
katanas, Jordan-style sneakers), localized skin tones. **Nagu**, **Biku**, **Fifu**. Central IP
— used on social, packaging, collectibles, murals, and the brand's "web verse."

## CONTENT FUNDAMENTALS

How NAGU writes. The voice is a **streetwear label announcing a surprise drop** — never a menu, never a corporate deck.

**Cadence — short, stacked, punchy.** Copy moves in clipped fragments, often in threes, frequently as period-separated staccato lines:
- *Burgers crave. Anime craze. Asian twist.*
- *Compact footprint. Big street presence.*
- *Bold flavor. Built different.*
- *Content. Culture. Community.*
- *Swap. Collect. Flex.*
- *Limited. Fast. Unpredictable.*

**Person.** Talks *to* the customer ("**You** deserve food that hits like a premium lifestyle experience") and *as* the brand collective ("**We** use anime culture. We do not copy it."). Confident, never pleading. Declarative, not questioning — though the deck uses a single bold question as a slide hook ("Why get in early?") immediately answered by a punch line.

**Casing.** Display headlines and shout lines run **ALL CAPS** (matching the distressed display face). Body and sub-copy are **sentence case**, clean and direct. Eyebrows / kickers are uppercase with wide tracking. Never title-case-everything; that reads corporate.

**Tone register.** Bold · Culturally Fluent · Kinetic · Collectible · Disciplined. Confident and a little cocky, but disciplined — it borrows anime's *edge and emotion* without childish overload. "Attitude, not overload."

**Dual-language layer.** English primary, with **subtle Japanese accents** for flavor — decorative katakana/kanji and paired callouts like **大胆な味 / Bold Flavor**. Arabic exists for market/legal use but stays out of the *graphic* system. Never crowd three languages into one layout.

**Emoji:** none. The energy comes from type, color, texture, and IP — not emoji. Unicode/“fun” glyphs are not part of the voice.

**Numbers & proof.** Used sparingly and concretely — prices in SR, outlet counts, the rollout roadmap. No vanity stats, no filler metrics.

**The brand IS:** street-pop lifestyle · high perceived value · visually explosive · dual-language (EN/JP) · culturally native · disciplined/lean · edgy · collectible · focused.
**The brand IS NOT:** childish cartoon overload · cheap/low-status · overdesigned · crowded 3-language layouts · corporate · loose · safe/generic · throwaway.

## VISUAL FOUNDATIONS

**Palette.** Three-color core: **NAGU Red `#B70D2A`**, **Near Black `#1E1E1E`**, **Off-White `#F2F2F2`**, with **Cream/Paper `#DCD2C8`** as warm support. Red is the loud accent and primary brand signal; black is the structural canvas and keyline; off-white/cream are the paper grounds. A sampled **kimono gold `#C8A24A`** appears as a rare premium/collectible accent (NAGU by CHAN, pins). High contrast always — red-on-black, black-on-cream, off-white-on-red. No pastels, no candy, no bluish-purple gradients.

**Type.** Three faces: **Nagu Display** (distressed, condensed, all-caps — every headline and poster shout), **Nagu Sans** (clean geometric — all body, UI, captions), **Nagu JP** (angular anime katakana/kanji — decorative dual-language accents only). Display runs *large and tight* (`line-height ~0.92`), stacked like a poster. Body stays calm and readable. The display face already carries grain/distress in its outlines, so it reads "printed poster" without extra filters.

**Backgrounds.** Three modes: (1) **Poster black** — full-bleed `#0C0C0C`/`#1E1E1E` for hero and drop moments; (2) **Paper** — off-white/cream for menus and content; (3) **Cinematic illustration** — the lifestyle art (Jeddah skyline at night drenched in red neon, street scenes). Grunge **texture overlays** (torn paper, concrete, halftone dots) are layered at low opacity (multiply / overlay) to add street grit. Never clean flat gradients as the main surface; the brand world is *printed and weathered.*

**The signature device — hard keyline + hard offset shadow.** Lifted straight from the wordmark: a **2–3px solid black keyline** around blocks, plus a **hard, no-blur offset block shadow** (`4px 4px 0 black`, sometimes red). This is the single most identifiable structural move. Cards, buttons, poster frames, price blocks all use it. Soft blurred shadows are reserved for floating UI on dark surfaces — used sparingly.

**Corners.** Mostly **square** (`radius 0`). Posters and takeaway windows are sharp-cornered. Small radii (`4–8px`) only on interactive controls; a `14px` max on pill-ish cards (sauce sachets, tags use full pills). Never big friendly SaaS rounding.

**Borders.** Hard black `2px` keylines are the default separator and frame. On dark surfaces, a low-opacity off-white hairline. Borders are part of the look, not just dividers.

**Imagery color vibe.** Product shots: **warm, glossy, high-contrast** on dark slate — dramatic and appetizing. Lifestyle/IP art: **saturated red neon over near-black**, cinematic anime rendering, occasional snow/particle atmosphere; warm skin tones. Everything skews **warm + high-contrast + a little gritty**, never cool/desaturated/corporate-stock.

**Motion.** Snappy and kinetic — `120–200ms`, ease `cubic-bezier(0.2,0.8,0.2,1)`. Quick fades and short slide-ins; **no slow floats, no bounce, no infinite decorative loops.** Energy comes from *snap*, matching the "drop" mentality.

**Hover.** Lift to brighter red (`--red-bright`), or invert (black block → red block). Hard-shadow elements can nudge `translate(-1px,-1px)` and deepen the shadow (a tactile "press-ready" lift).
**Press / active.** Shrink slightly (`scale(0.98)`) and/or move *into* the shadow (`translate(2px,2px)` with shadow collapsing) — the block "stamps down." Color deepens to `--red-deep`.

**Transparency & blur.** Used rarely — a dark scrim over imagery for legibility (gradient protection bottom-up on hero photos), or a subtle blur behind a floating order bar. The brand prefers solid opaque blocks over glassy translucency; blur is a tool, not a theme.

**Cards.** Square or near-square corners, solid paper or ink fill, **hard black keyline + hard offset shadow**. No subtle drop-shadow "floating card" look on light surfaces — that's too soft/corporate. Product cards often pair a photo block with a bold display name and a price tag rendered as a stamped block.

**Layout rules.** Poster-grade hierarchy: one dominant display element, generous black/paper negative space, tight 8px grid. Fixed elements: a sticky top bar (logo left), and on ordering surfaces a fixed bottom order/cart bar. Dual-language accents pinned as small vertical/edge callouts. Compact, dense, decisive — *small space, maximum impact.*

## ICONOGRAPHY

NAGU was **not** delivered with a custom icon font, SVG icon set, or sprite sheet — the brand
package is type-, color-, texture-, and IP-led. The visual "icons" of the brand are really its
**assets**: the graffiti wordmark, the three mascots, paint-stroke marks, and product cut-outs.

**Approach in this system:**
- **UI icons** use **[Lucide](https://lucide.dev)** loaded from CDN — a clean, consistent
  `2px`-stroke open set that matches NAGU's hard-keyline geometry. This is a **documented
  substitution** (no brand icon set existed); swap for a bespoke set when one is produced.
  Stroke `2–2.25px`, square line caps preferred, colored `currentColor` (ink or red).
- **No emoji**, ever — off-brand.
- **Brand marks as icons:** the paint-stroke elements (`assets/elements/paint-stroke-0*.png`)
  act as underline/highlight/stamp accents. The secondary one-color wordmark works as an app
  glyph / favicon-scale mark.
- **Mascot IP** is the brand's richest "iconography" — use the character portraits as avatars,
  loyalty stamps, and collectible cards rather than inventing pictograms.
- **Dual-language tags** (decorative katakana/kanji) function as ornamental glyphs in `Nagu JP`.

Never hand-draw bespoke SVG illustrations or generate mascots — use the real assets in `assets/`.
